Depletion of resources

As the world’s population increases, we are using more and more of the earth’s resources to support our current living standards. Resources are either renewable or non-renewable.

Renewable resource

A renewable resource is a resource that is practically inexhaustible or can be regenerated and recycled. Using renewable sources of energy promotes sustainable living and these sources are virtually pollution free.

Some examples of renewable resources are sun, wind and water.

close

Non-renewable resources

A non-renewable resource is a finite resource that will eventually run out. Some examples of non-renewable resources are coal, oil and gas.

In addition, the extraction of these resources threatens unique habitats with extinction, damages our eco-system and releases carbon dioxide (a greenhouse gas) into the atmosphere when burnt.
