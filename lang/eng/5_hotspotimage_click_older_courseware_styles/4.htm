On an individual basis, there are lots of ways you can reduce energy usage on a daily basis.
Click below on the areas where energy usage could be reduced. (Hint: there are 8)

Computer

Correct

Remember, using electricity releases Carbon Dioxide, a greenhouse gas, into the atmosphere. Some very simple ways to reduce Carbon Dioxide emissions is to turn off lights and appliances when not in use. We all do it without thinking; leave appliances on stand-by instead of turning them off. Appliances most likely to be left on stand-by include TVs, PCs and phone chargers. Equipment on stand-by uses up to 20% of the energy it uses when fully on.

Window

Correct

A great way to conserve energy required to provide lighting is to use natural light wherever possible. Switch off lights when you leave a room. Don't waste energy lighting unoccupied rooms. Use timers to control the lights if you need them for security.

Kettle

Correct

Always make efficient use of energy to save resources and reduce costs. When boiling a kettle, only boil the amount of water you need. Don’t fill up the whole kettle if you are only going to make one cup of tea!

Lightbulb

Correct

Keep light bulbs and tubes clean. Replace existing bulbs with compact fluorescent lamps (CFLs). CFL light bulbs only use 20% of the energy a traditional bulb uses and last up to 15 times as long.

Dishwasher

Correct

Energy efficient appliances cost less to run and are less harmful to the environment. Energy consumption is rated from A-G with A being the most efficient. Always use your dishwater with a full load as a half full one uses the same energy as a fully loaded one.

Open door

Correct

A simple way to conserve energy used to provide heat is to keep all windows and doors closed when the heating or air-conditioning is on.

Thermostat

Correct

Turn your heating down to a comfortable 20 degrees centigrade. Simple things like reducing the temperature of a room by even 1 degree centigrade can save 10% on your heating bill. Turn off the heating when you're not there. You should also ensure your house (attic, windows, walls) and hot water cylinders are properly insulated to provide maximum performance from your heating system.

Radiator

Correct

Fit each radiator with a thermostatic valve so your room temperature can be controlled. Ensure your heating systems are serviced regularly and well maintained. To minimise environmental impacts, choose natural gas when installing a heating system in preference to oil fired systems.
