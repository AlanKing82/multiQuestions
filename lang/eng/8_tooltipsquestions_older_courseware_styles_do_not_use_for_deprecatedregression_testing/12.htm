Case Studies

Acts of bribery and corruption have been criminalised, the law enforcement authorities and the courts now take them very seriously and penalties include large fines and even imprisonment, Consider the examples below by clicking on each box:

A Telecoms Company
An executive negotiated a US$50 million payment to cover up bribes paid in Saudi Arabia
The company has paid out over US$1.6 billion in fines in Germany and the US, and over US$1 billion to lawyers and accountants for its own investigations.
Star deal maker fired
A star deal maker at an investment bank was fired, and his supervisor was placed on administrative leave after discovering that he may have secured some transactions by offering cash or gifts to Chinese officials.
Broker made "corrupt payments"
They were fined £5.25 million by the Financial Services Authority as a mitigated fine for failings in its anti-bribery and corruption systems. The FSA stated that 66 suspicious payments amounting to approximately US$7 million were paid to overseas third parties in Ghana, Nigeria, Philippines, Burma, Bahrain, Bangladesh, Bulgaria, Indonesia and Vietnam between 2005 and 2007 to "win or retain business."
