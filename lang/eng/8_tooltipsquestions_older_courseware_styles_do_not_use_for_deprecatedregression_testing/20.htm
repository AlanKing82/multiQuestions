Once you are sitting back in your chair, you now need to set the chair at the correct height for you. This involves addressing 3 key areas.

Forearms and wrists

Your chair is at the correct height when your forearms and wrists are horizontal with the keyboard. To achieve this, move your chair close enough to the desk so that you can reach the keyboard comfortably. (Ideally, place the keyboard at least 10cm from the edge of the desk).

If your chair is equipped with armrests, they must not prevent you from moving close to your desk. Additionally, adjust armrests to ensure that they properly support your forearms and prevent hunching of the shoulders.

close

Feet flat on the floor

With your arms and wrists in the correct position, check that your feet are flat on the floor.

If this is not the case, you may be able to adjust the height of your desk or support your feet by using a footrest.

close

Head position

With your forearms and feet in the correct position, adjust the screen so that your eyes are roughly at the same height as the top of the screen.

Generally, your screen height and angle should allow for a comfortable head position that reduces the need for awkward up and down movements of the head.
