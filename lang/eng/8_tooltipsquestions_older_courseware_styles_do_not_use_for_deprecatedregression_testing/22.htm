Stress

Stress associated with workstation use is related to the following areas:

  • Task design.
  • Workstation set-up.
  • The environment in which you work.

Task design

Stress associated with task design include aspects such as:

  • Lack of control over work
  • Skills not being utilised
  • No training or consultation with the user
  • High-speed repetitive tasks
  • High workloads combined with tight deadlines
  • Use of inappropriate software and/or lack of adequate training on how to use the software provided.
close

Workstation set-up

A workstation that is not set-up correctly or a workstation with inappropriate equipment can give rise to unnecessary stress and frustration.

Stress associated with the working environment include aspects such as:

  • Working in isolation
  • Working in an excessively loud workspace
  • Working in uncomfortable temperatures (hot or cold)
