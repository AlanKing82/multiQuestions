Forearms and wrists horizontal

Next, set your chair at the correct height for you. To do this, move your chair close enough to the desk so that you can reach the keyboard comfortably. (It is recommended that your keyboard should be set back at least 10cm from the edge of the desk). Now, check that in this position your forearms and wrists are horizontal with the keyboard.

If your chair is equipped with armrests, the armrests must not prevent you from moving close to your desk.

Adjust armrests to ensure that they properly support your forearms and prevent hunching of the shoulders.

Feet flat on the floor

With your arms and wrists in the correct position, check that your feet are flat on the floor.

If this is not the case, you may be able to adjust the height of your desk or support your feet by using a footrest.

Head position

With your forearms horizontal to the keyboard and your feet flat on the floor, your eyes should now be roughly at the same height as the top of the screen.

Generally, your screen height and angle should allow for a comfortable head position that restrict awkward up and down movements of the head.
